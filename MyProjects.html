My Dissertation from my final year of my BSc (Hons) Computer Science degree which I completed at Heriot Watt University.
Formula 1 is a data-driven sport, with large amounts of data being gathered and analysed to give competitors the edge over one another to achieve the goal of winning World Championships. This project will explore graph databases and visualisation techniques to model the dataset in Neo4j, and explore the visualisations provided to analyse the dataset and discuss the advantages and disadvantages of using graph databases for analysis of the dataset.



Edinburgh Airport Flight Information for August 2017
Using some raw data containing information about flights which arrived or departed from Edinburgh Airport, I created a report analysing this data to produce a monthly summary of passenger flight information illustrating trends on passenger distributions, aircraft delays, seat load factors, pier service levels, and passenger distributions across departure gates and baggage reclaim belts. This data analysis has been carried out using Microsoft Excel, with the report being produced with Microsoft Word. Furthermore, I have created a visualisation of this raw data in PowerBI to provide another method of representing this data.

I am a BSc (Hons) Computer Science graduate who is currently working as a data analyst in the energy sector. I joined the sector in May 2021 and have been analysing large amounts of electricity data, from a variety of different energy assets, through a turbulent time of energy prices and the industry. Having just come out of record lows from the coronavirus pandemic, prices increased to record highs due to the global gas price and Russian invasion of Ukraine, with the UK government needing to step in to shield domestic and non-domestic price surges.

I am passionate about data and enjoy analysing any information I can, in the hope of gathering useful insights which can be presented, to allow for useful and beneficial decisions to be made based on the analysis.

I now wish to use the skills and knowledge I have gathered throughout my educational and work experience to help those to invest in renewable energy systems for their household, to protect the environment and provide savings from the electricity bills. I can also help with existing solar and battery storage systems within your home, analyse the data currently being produced by your systems, and make sure that you are benefitting from the highest levels of revenue you can.

I will never take any commission from any contracts that you agree as part of my analysis. All of my fee structures are simple and easy to understand before I carry out any work for you, so you can be sure of the cost of my analysis before I start the work. I operate on a fixed fee basis, so you know exactly how much this will cost you before you make the decision. I invoice all of my work upon completion.

For my analysis, I will require some simple details about your system, such as capacity and the systems installed. I will also require a minimum of 12 months (or less, if your system is less than 12 months old) of half-hourly data from your system to allow me to carry out my analysis. I can advise how this can be downloaded, or I can be added as an authorised party to your account which will allow me to download all of the data myself. I may also require a Single Line Diagram of your Battery Storage system, if applicable.
